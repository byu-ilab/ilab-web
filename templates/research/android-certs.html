Android Certs

The Internet Research lab is conducting a study on man-in-the-middle attacks. To do this, our Android application will collect some basic information when your phone makes a secure connection to a website. This includes:

  • the website's certificate (which is public information),
  • what city you are in, and
  • the IP address of your phone at the time of connection.

We will use this information to determine if you have been given a valid certificate. If the certificate is not valid, we will warn you that you are likely being attacked and your traffic is visible to the attacker. If this occurs, you should be careful not to share personal information, such as your credit card number, with any sites.

We will not release your IP address or any other personally identifiable information to any party.

For details and questions, please contact Dr. Daniel Zappala at zappala@byu.edu
