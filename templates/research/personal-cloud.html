Personal Cloud Computing

Vision of the cloud ecosystem

Imagine a personal cloud connecting all of your mobile and home computing devices, providing seamless and transparent access to all of your content, regardless of where it is stored. Don't remember whether you have that video stored on your video camera, phone, or laptop? A personal cloud would help you search through all your videos on all your devices. The personal cloud will become increasingly desirable as we continue filling our homes with a variety of computing devices, and as the Internet-of-things becomes a reality.

Why aren't commercial cloud providers such as Dropbox sufficient?

  • Performance — you can stream videos to your TV much faster from a local cloud.
  • Cost — ISPs are imposing bandwidth caps and overage charges, making it infeasible to store all your data in a commercial cloud provider.
  • Security — do you trust Google to manage your data, even if it is encrypted, when the NSA is collecting so much of our information?
  • Control — you can decide where your data is stored, what level of encryption to use, and what pieces you backup to commercial providers.

Our work is in the early stages of exploring how to build a personal cloud and its ramifications for distributed systems, security, and usability.
