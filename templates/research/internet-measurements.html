Internet Measurements

Measuring the Internet provides insight into not just our global infrastructure but also the people who use it.

In popular media gamers are often portrayed as "male and young, pale from too much time spent indoors, and socially inept...a new generation of isolated and lonely 'couch potatoes,' [who] are far from aspirational figures" (Who plays, how much, and why? Debunking the stereotypical gamer profile, Dimitri Williams, Nick Yee, and Scott Caplan). Such views are further reinforced by stereotypes commonly held by both gsociety as a whole and the gaming culture itself.

Our current project seeks to understand the diverse behavior of gamers by measuring the hundred million users of the Steam online gaming platform. We are in the process of publishing our findings from this study.
