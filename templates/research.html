Current Research

Personal Cloud Computing

Imagine a personal cloud connecting all of your mobile and home computing devices, providing seamless and transparent access to all of your content, regardless of where it is stored. This would complement existing cloud services such as Dropbox by providing higher peformance, lower cost, greater security, and stronger control over your data. Our research is in the early stages of exploring how to build a personal cloud and its ramifications for distributed systems, security, and usability.

Sentiment Analysis of Political Tweets

Twitter has played a prominent role in providing a platform for political and social change in the Middle East. We are exploring how to automatically classify the political sentiment of tweets, so that we can better quantify political feelings of people around the world and track their changes over time.

WiFu: A Composable Toolkit for Experimental Wireless Protocols

A long-standing problem in wireless networks is how to provide efficient and fair transport over multiple wireless hops. To encourage more experimental research in this area, we have designed an open source, user space toolkit that enables researchers to build experimental transport protocols without modifying the Linux kernel. We are also examining improvements and alternatives to TCP.

Wireless Network Modeling and Mapping

We are studying how to provide fair rate control in wireless mesh networks, by characterizing the interference and contention among network devices. This work is done in collaboration with the IDeA Labs at BYU, providing collaboration on systems and control theory as it applies to networking.

Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP

We are developing software and algorithms to improve the performance of streaming video over the Internet. We have developed simulation software that enables researchers to study convergence and stability of these systems. We have also shown that incorporating scalable video coding provides greater flexibility for clients to adjust quality when bandwidth is highly variable.
